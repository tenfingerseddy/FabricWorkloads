# FabCon 2026 Engagement Plan

> Event: Microsoft Fabric Community Conference (FabCon) + SQLCon
> Dates: March 16-20, 2026
> Location: Georgia World Congress Center, Atlanta, GA
> Our Status: Remote (not attending in person)
> Goal: Maximize visibility during the highest-traffic week in the Fabric community

---

## Strategy

FabCon is the single biggest attention spike in the Fabric ecosystem all year. 200+ sessions, thousands of attendees, massive social media activity. Even without attending in person, we can ride the wave by:

1. Publishing timely content that references FabCon announcements
2. Engaging with live session discussions on LinkedIn and Reddit
3. Responding to pain points surfaced during sessions
4. Positioning our tool as relevant to announced features/gaps

## Pre-FabCon (March 14-15)

### LinkedIn Post (Already in week-02-social-posts.md)
- FabCon preview post mentioning observability gaps and hoping for announcements
- Links to our GitHub repo

### Reddit
- Monitor r/MicrosoftFabric for pre-event discussion threads
- Comment helpfully on any monitoring/observability threads

## During FabCon (March 16-20)

### Real-Time Monitoring (Daily)
- Check #FabCon2026 and #MicrosoftFabric on LinkedIn/X
- Monitor Fabric blog (blog.fabric.microsoft.com) for announcements
- Watch r/MicrosoftFabric for live session recaps
- Track any monitoring/observability announcements

### Reactive Content (Within 24h of announcements)

**If Microsoft announces monitoring improvements:**
LinkedIn post: "Great to see [specific improvement] announced at FabCon. Here's how it fits into the broader observability picture and what gaps remain."

**If Microsoft announces capacity/cost tools:**
LinkedIn post: "The new [feature] addresses [specific pain point]. For teams that also need [gap our tool fills], here's how the open-source Observability Workbench complements it."

**If no monitoring announcements:**
LinkedIn post: "FabCon 2026 brought incredible announcements for [areas]. The observability gap remains an opportunity for the community to solve. Here's what we're building."

### Session-Specific Engagement

Sessions to monitor for relevance:
- Any session mentioning "monitoring," "observability," or "monitoring hub"
- Capacity management and cost optimization sessions
- Data quality and governance sessions
- Pipeline reliability and CI/CD sessions
- Any Extensibility Toolkit / workload development sessions

When a relevant session is discussed online:
- Engage with the discussion authentically (add value, share experience)
- Reference our blog posts when directly relevant
- Do NOT hard-sell; provide genuine insight first

## Metrics to Track

- LinkedIn post impressions during FabCon week vs. normal week
- GitHub repo visits/stars during March 16-22
- Reddit post engagement on FabCon-related threads
- Any inbound inquiries or issues opened on the repo

## Key Rules

1. **Be genuinely helpful.** Every interaction adds value first. Product mention second (or not at all).
2. **Be timely.** FabCon content has a 48-hour relevance window. Publish fast or not at all.
3. **Reference specific sessions/announcements.** Generic "great event!" posts are invisible. Specific reactions get engagement.
4. **Acknowledge Microsoft's progress.** Position as "building on top of" not "replacing" native tools.
5. **Link to GitHub, not a landing page.** Engineers trust open source. Let the code speak.
