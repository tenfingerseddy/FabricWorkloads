The Problem

Fabric monitoring has critical blind spots

Microsoft Fabric gives you powerful analytics, but its built-in monitoring leaves platform teams in the dark when it matters most.

30-Day Retention Limit

Fabric's built-in monitoring data expires after 30 days. Capacity planning, trend analysis, and audit compliance require months or years of history — history you simply don't have.

No Cross-Item Correlation

A slow pipeline triggers a failed refresh that breaks a report. But in Fabric, these are three separate, disconnected events. Finding the root cause means hours of manual detective work.

No SLO Tracking

You can't define or track service level objectives. When leadership asks "What's our data freshness SLA?" you're stuck exporting CSVs and building manual dashboards that are already stale.

The Solution

Everything Fabric monitoring should have been

Observability Workbench extends Fabric with the monitoring, alerting, and analysis capabilities that enterprise data teams need.

Long-Retention Store

Every metric, event, and log persisted for as long as you need. Built on Fabric Lakehouse storage so costs stay predictable and data never leaves your tenant.

Correlation Engine

Automatically links related events across pipelines, datasets, lakehouses, and reports. See the full causal chain, not isolated symptoms.

SLO Tracking

Define service level objectives for freshness, availability, and performance. Track error budgets in real-time and know before your users do.

Proactive Alerts

Threshold, anomaly, and SLO-burn-rate alerts delivered to Teams, email, or PagerDuty. Stop finding out about outages from your stakeholders.

Incident Timelines

When something breaks, get a chronological, correlated timeline of every event that contributed. Cut mean-time-to-resolution from hours to minutes.

Full-Text Search

Search across every log message, error trace, and event payload. Find that one failed refresh from three months ago in seconds, not hours.

How It Works

Up and running in minutes, not weeks

Step 3

Define SLOs and alert rules. Get notified in Teams, email, or PagerDuty before issues reach your stakeholders.
