FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Everything you need to know about pricing, billing, and how Observability Workbench integrates with your Fabric environment.

A "capacity" refers to a single Microsoft Fabric capacity (e.g., F2, F64, F128). Each Fabric capacity is an independent compute unit that hosts your workspaces. Our per-capacity pricing means you pay based on how many Fabric capacities you monitor, not how many individual workspaces or items are inside them. If you have one F64 capacity with 20 workspaces, that counts as one capacity.
Yes, you can upgrade or downgrade at any time. Upgrades take effect immediately and you'll be prorated for the remaining billing period. Downgrades take effect at the start of your next billing cycle. When downgrading, your data retention will adjust to the new tier's limit, so we recommend exporting any data you need to keep beyond the new retention window.
Yes. The Professional tier includes a 14-day free trial with full feature access. No credit card is required to start. At the end of the trial, you can choose to continue on the Professional plan or stay on the Free tier without losing any data collected during the trial period (subject to Free tier retention limits). For Enterprise trials, contact our sales team to arrange a custom evaluation.
We never cut off monitoring without warning. If you hit your workspace limit, you'll receive an in-app notification and email prompting you to upgrade. Existing workspaces continue to be monitored normally. For retention limits, data older than your tier's retention window is automatically archived to your Fabric Lakehouse before being removed from the active store, so nothing is permanently lost. SLO limits on the Free tier are a soft cap with a 7-day grace period to give you time to upgrade.
All monitoring data stays within your own Microsoft Fabric tenant. Observability Workbench uses Fabric Eventhouse for real-time querying and Fabric Lakehouse for long-term archival. No data ever leaves your tenant boundary or is sent to third-party services. This architecture ensures compliance with your organization's data residency and sovereignty requirements.
Observability Workbench works with any Fabric capacity SKU from F2 and above. However, we recommend F64 or higher for production deployments to ensure the workload's background ingestion and correlation jobs have sufficient compute headroom alongside your regular workloads. The Free tier works well with Fabric trial capacities for evaluation purposes.
